Questions

After completing the lab recap as a class the following questions:

General Discussion Questions

Q: What are some of the industry trends pushing companies towards transformation?
A:

  • Software Eating the world - every company need to become a software company to stay relevant/in business
  • Mobile apps/ different devices
  • IT not being able to keep with business expectations

Q: What are the benefits of Pivotal Cloud Foundry?
A:

  • IaaS Independence
  • Self Serve Platform for Developers (cf push, services)
  • Operationally Robust (HA, Metrics, Logs, Quotas, etc)

Q: What did you just do?
A: Deployed 4 apps.

Q: What does push mean?
A: Deploy an app.

Q: What was the same?
A: The command. Regardless of language you ended up with a running app.

Q: What was different?
A: URI, language, the buildpack

Q: Is this easier than what you normally do?

Q: How long does this take you now?

Questions From the Lab

Q: What are some common items in the output that occurred when pushing each application?
A: loading buildpacks, creating containers, creating routes, etc. The goal here is to start learning the vocabulary, but also generate excitement about how easy PCF makes things.

Troubleshooting

Troubleshooting guidance for the lab that instructors need to know.

In the initial release of PCF 1.7 the Apps Manager shipped with the wrong/old version of the cf CLI (6.13). At least version 6.16 is needed to do these labs (Service Broker and Route Service). The latest stable cf CLI can be downloaded from https://github.com/cloudfoundry/cli#downloads. Students will need to download from this location rather than the Apps Manager.
